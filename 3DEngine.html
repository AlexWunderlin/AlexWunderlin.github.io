3D Engine

My 3D engine is a simple program on scratch that renders a list of 3D coordinates. The user controls the camera which they can move throughout environment and rotate to face different directions.

Inspiration

Making a 3D game was something I had always wanted to do since I first started making video games. To make a 3D game, I first needed to learn how to display things in 3D, so I began development on a simple 3D program. It didn't need to be anything fancy, just a starting point I could later improve upon. It was important that the program I made was something I could call my own, hence I avoided tutorials and other resources.

How it Works

The basic idea comes down to how 3D points are converted to 2D points that can be displayed on a screen. Imagine two points, one to represent the camera and one to represent a point to be displayed. Now imagine a plane in front of the camera, perpendicular to the ray representing the direction the camera is facing. On that plane lies a coordinate grid with the origin being where it is intersected by the ray. By connecting the camera and the other point with a line, you can find the point's 2D location on the screen using the 2D coordinate of where that line intersects the plane. Note that in this example (0, 0) is located in the center of the screen. Obviously anything with a 2D coordinate outside the bounds of the screen would not be displayed. The distance of the plane from the camera and the scale of the coordinate grid can vary and affects the field of view and zoom.
